Example1.1Expanding logarithmic expressions

Expand the following using the properties of logarithms and simplify. Assume when necessary that all quantities represent positive real numbers.

Interestingly enough, expanding logarithms is the exact opposite process (which we will practice later) that is most useful in Algebra. The utility of expanding logarithms becomes apparent in Calculus.

  1. \(\log_{2}\left(\dfrac{8}{x}\right)\)

  2. \(\log_{0.1} \left(10 x^2 \right)\)

  3. \(\ln \left(\dfrac{3}{ex}\right)^2\)

  4. \(\log \sqrt[3]{\dfrac{100 x^2}{yz^5}}\)

  5. \(\log_{117}\left(x^2 - 4\right)\)
