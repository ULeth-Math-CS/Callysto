Let \(f(x) = b^{x}\) and \(g(x) = \log_{b}(x)\) where \(b>0\text{,}\) \(b\neq 1\text{.}\) Then \(f\) and \(g\) are one-to-one and

  • \(b^{u} = b^{w}\) if and only if \(u=w\) for all real numbers \(u\) and \(w\text{.}\)

  • \(\log_{b}(u) = \log_{b}(w)\) if and only if \(u=w\) for all real numbers \(u > 0\text{,}\) \(w > 0\text{.}\)
