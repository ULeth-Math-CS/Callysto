Item5

At first it seems as if we have no means of simplifying \(\log_{117}\left(x^2-4\right)\text{,}\) since none of the properties of logs addresses the issue of expanding a difference inside the logarithm. However, we may factor \(x^2 - 4 = (x+2)(x-2)\) thereby introducing a product which gives us license to use the Product Rule.

\begin{align*} \log_{117}\left(x^2-4\right) \amp = \log_{117} \left[(x+2)(x-2)\right] \tag{Factor}\\ \amp = \log_{117}(x+2) + \log_{117}(x-2) \tag{Product Rule} \end{align*}
