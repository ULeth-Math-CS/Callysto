Let \(g(x) =\log_{b}(x)\) be a logarithmic function (\(b > 0\text{,}\) \(b\neq 1\)) and let \(u>0\) and \(w>0\) be real numbers.

  • Product Rule: \(g(uw) = g(u)+ g(w)\text{.}\) In other words, \(\log_{b}(uw) = \log_{b}(u) + \log_{b}(w)\)

  • Quotient Rule: \(g\left(\dfrac{u}{w} \right) = g(u) - g(w)\text{.}\) In other words, \(\log_{b} \left( \dfrac{u}{w} \right) = \log_{b}(u) - \log_{b}(w)\)

  • Power Rule: \(g\left(u^{w}\right) =w g(u)\text{.}\) In other words, \(\log_{b}\left(u^{w}\right) = w \log_{b}(u)\)
